Our study aims to understand how people use multimedia content during a crisis. We selected 18 research articles to analyze what previous researchers did. We reduced the number of articles to 12 because some of them are not relevant to our topic. Our research proposes an AI-based tool for content analysis during crises. We hope to identify misinformation and emotions behind content. Following are the previous researches and their methods and findings.

7. Exploratory Analysis of a Social Media Network in Sri Lanka during the COVID-19 Virus Outbreak

Amidst the global upheaval caused by the COVID-19 pandemic, Sri Lanka, situated in South Asia, witnessed a series of events influenced by the virus outbreak. In response to these events, the people of Sri Lanka turned to social media platforms, particularly Facebook, to express their opinions and reactions. This presented an opportunity to delve into the social perceptions and behaviors of the Sri Lankan population during this crisis.

Methods:

Posts related to the COVID-19 outbreak in Sri Lanka on Facebook, Period extended one month after the initial major outbreak in the country and Including categorization, assessment of behavioral patterns, identification of significant topics, and analysis of topic co-occurrences, were applied to the collected data.

Findings:

Detrimental ideas were shared on social media, the majority of posts conveyed constructive and positive thoughts. This suggests a significant influence from the cultural and social values that Sri Lankan society promotes. These values were pivotal in shaping the public discourse during the crisis.

8. Grappling with the COVID-19 Health Crisis: Content Analysis of Communication Strategies and Their Effects on Public Engagement on Social Media

Social media offer innovative opportunities for people to feel and stay socially connected in difficult times. Food is an essential part of daily life, how users use food and social media in times of crisis has not been investigated. Food photos on social media are generally intended to be visually appealing. This has created the ‘Instagrammable’ culture with photos on social media are that must look pretty and be enviable images. Information sharing on social media allows users to control what they share. The content created can be an extension of themselves and provide a sense of belonging. Social media use allows people to establish and sustain relationships based on their desire to belong.

Methods:

608 COVID-19 posts using content analysis. It is focused on content, message style, and interactive features. The content dimension was divided into six sub-dimensions, covering aspects such as action, new evidence, reassurance, disease prevention, health care services, and uncertainty. The style dimension was categorized into narrative and non-narrative styles, while interactive features included elements like links to external sources, hashtags, questions to feedback, and the use of multimedia. Public engagement was measured by tracking shares, comments, and likes on. Different communication strategies influenced public engagement levels.

Findings:

Government COVID-19 communication emphasized content frames like action, new evidence, and reassurance. However, posts related to new evidence and presented in a non-narrative style had a negative impact on the number of shares. Posts focused on disease prevention and delivered in a narrative style were highly effective in generating shares. An interaction effect was observed between content and style, with the use of a narrative style in disease prevention posts significantly increasing comments and likes. Including links to external sources positively contributed to sharing.

9. Concerns Expressed by Chinese Social Media Users during the COVID-19 Pandemic: Content Analysis of Sine Weibo Microblogging Data

In the COVID-19 pandemic, People turned to social media platforms like Twitter and Sina Weibo for information and support. These platforms have played crucial roles in facilitating communication and sharing of information and opinions among individuals, governments, and organizations

Methods:

On Sina Weibo, China's largest social media platform, to investigate the main concerns expressed by its users during the COVID-19 pandemic. The collected text and employed a topic modeling technique called latent Dirichlet allocation (LDA) to identify the most common topics discussed by users. The study analyzed the emotional tone of these topics, assessed their distribution, conducted user behavior analysis based on likes, comments, and retweets, and examined regional differences in user concerns within mainland China.

Findings:

The study identified 17 topics grouped into 8 themes from a pool of 203,191 eligible microblog posts. These topics covered various aspects of the pandemic, including statistics, domestic and global epidemics, treatments, medical resources, economic impacts, quarantine measures, psychological effects, and more. Of these topics, 11 had a predominantly positive sentiment, while 6 leaned toward a negative sentiment. Notably, the topic "domestic epidemic" received the highest average number of retweets, while "quarantine and investigation" garnered the most likes.
